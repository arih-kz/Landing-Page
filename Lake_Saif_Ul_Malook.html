The lake is accessible from Naran during the summer season, but access is limited in winter due to heavy snowfall and landslides. Saiful Muluk was formed by glacial moraines that blocked the stream passing through the valley. The Kaghan Valley was formed in the Pleistocene Period, almost 300,000 years ago, when the area was covered with ice. Melting water collected into the lake.

The lake has rich eco-diversity and hosts many species of blue-green algae. Large brown trout, up to about seven kilograms, are found in the lake. Approximately 26 species of vascular plants exist in the area, with Asteraceae being the most common. Other species include Ranunculaceae, Compositae, Cruciferae, Gramineae, Apiaceae, Leguminosae, Scrophulariaceae, and Polygonaceae.

The lake is named after a legendary prince. A fairy tale called Saiful Muluk, later turned into a poem by the Sufi poet Mian Muhammad Bakhsh, tells the story of the Egyptian Prince Saiful Malook who fell in love with a fairy princess named Princess Badri-ul-Jamala at the lake.
