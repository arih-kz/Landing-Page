The palace was constructed using pink Jodhpur stone and local yellow stone from nearby Gizri. It features nine domes, with a centre dome, and windows with stained glass in an arched style. The "barsati" (terrace) included a family temple dedicated to Lord Shiva (Hindu God).

In 1995, the Government of Sindh purchased the palace for conversion into a museum. A trust was established to manage the property, ensuring it would not be sold or used for commercial purposes. The Mohatta Palace Museum officially opened in 1999.
