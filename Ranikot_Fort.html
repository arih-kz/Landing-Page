Ranikot Fort is located 90 kilometers north of Hyderabad on the Indus Highway (N55). It is accessible via a rugged 21-kilometer road from Sann, the nearest town, and is within the Kirthar National Park, the second-largest national park in Pakistan. The fort's original purpose and architects are unknown. It was once thought to be built by the Sassanians, Scythians, Parthians, or Bactrian Greeks, but recent evidence suggests it originated under the Talpurs, specifically during the administration of Nawab Wali Muhammed Leghari, the Prime Minister of Sindh.

Archaeologists now date its first construction to the 17th century, with significant reconstruction by the Talpur dynasty in 1812. Radiocarbon tests confirm renovations of the Sann Gate occurred between the early 18th and early 19th centuries. The fort, measuring 31 kilometers in length, connects several mountains of the Kirthar Hills. It features semi-circular bastions and a zig-zag wall. Inside the main fort is the smaller Miri Fort, believed to have served as the palace of the Mir royal family. The fort has four entry gates: Sann Gate, Amri Gate, Shah-Pere Gate, and Mohan Gate.
