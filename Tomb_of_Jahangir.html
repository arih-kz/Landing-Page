The tomb was built in Jahangir's pleasure garden, the Dilkusha Garden, which had been laid out in 1557. The Tomb of Asif Khan and the Akbari Sarai are located immediately west of Jahangir's tomb complex. The tomb of Jahangir's wife Nur Jahan is slightly southwest of Asif Khan's tomb.

Jahangir, who ruled from 1605 to 1627, died in Kashmir on 28 October 1627. His body was brought to Lahore on 12 November 1627, and he was buried in the Dilkusha Garden, a favorite spot of his. His son, Shah Jahan, ordered the construction of the mausoleum in a Mughal style influenced by Safavid architecture. The tomb is unique in that it lacks a central dome, which Jahangir reportedly forbade.

Construction started in 1627, took ten years, and cost Rs 10 Lakh. The tomb complex was desecrated and pillaged under Sikh rule, with materials used for the Golden Temple in Amritsar. Further desecration occurred when Musa Sahib was buried on the tomb's grounds in 1828.

The site suffered further damage under British rule when a railway line was built between the tombs of Asif Khan and Nur Jahan. The site has also been repeatedly threatened by flooding from the Ravi River, notably in 1988 when the site was submerged under 10 feet of water for five days.
