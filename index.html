About Pakistan

Pakistan officially known as the Islamic Republic Of Pakistan, is a country located in South Asia. It shares borders with India to the East, Afghanistan and Iran to the West, China to the North, and the Arabian Sea to the South. Pakistan is the Fifth-most Populous country in the world, with a diverse culture and rich history.

History:

Independence: Pakistan gained independence on August 14, 1947, following the end of British colonial rule in the Indian subcontinent. The country was created as a separate state for Muslims in the region, resulting from the partition of British India into two independent dominions: India and Pakistan.

Founding Father: "Muhammad Ali Jinnah" is considered the father of the nation.

Partition: Pakistan initially consisted of two geographically and culturally distinct regions—West Pakistan (now Pakistan) and East Pakistan (now Bangladesh).

Geography:

Capital: "Islamabad."

Major Cities: "Karachi", "Lahore", "Faisalabad", "Rawalpindi", "Peshawar", and "Quetta".

Regions: "Punjab", "Sindh", "Khyber Pakhtunkhwa", "Balochistan", "Gilgit-Baltistan", and "Azad Jammu & Kashmir".

Culture:

Language: "Urdu" is the national language, while "English" is widely used for official and business purposes. Regional languages include "Punjabi", "Sindhi", "Pashto", and "Balochi".

Cuisine: Pakistani cuisine is known for its rich flavors and diverse dishes, including "biryani", "kebabs", "nihari", and "various" types of bread like "naan" and "roti".

Clothing: Traditional clothing includes "shalwar kameez" for both men and women, often adorned with intricate embroidery.

Music and Art: Pakistan has a vibrant tradition of music, including "qawwali", "ghazals", and "folk music". The country also has a rich heritage of "visual arts", "calligraphy", and "architecture".

Economy:

Industries: Major industries include "textiles", "agriculture", "manufacturing", and "services". Pakistan is known for its textile products, which are exported worldwide.

Currency: "Pakistani Rupee" (PKR)

Tourism:

Heritage Sites: Pakistan is home to numerous UNESCO World Heritage Sites, including the ancient city of "Mohenjo-Daro", "Lahore Fort", and "Rohtas Fort".

Natural Beauty: The northern regions of Pakistan are famous for their stunning landscapes, including the "Karakoram" and "Himalayan" mountain ranges, and attractions like "Hunza Valley", "Fairy Meadows", and "Lake Saiful Muluk".

Sports:

Hockey: Field hockey is pakistan's national game. Field hockey is also a significant sport, with Pakistan historically being one of the top teams in the world.

Cricket: Cricket is the most popular sport in Pakistan, with a strong following for both domestic and international matches.

Religion:

Islam: The majority of Pakistan's population is Muslim, with Islam being the state religion. The country has a significant Sufi tradition, and religious festivals like "Eid-ul-Fitr" and "Eid-ul-Adha" are widely celebrated.

Let's Celebrate Pakistan!

landmarks

A landmark literally meant a geographic feature used by explorers and others to find their way back or through an area. Landmarks are important or well-known places, buildings, or natural features that help people recognize or remember a location.

Types:

Landmarks are usually classified as either natural landmarks or human-made landmarks, both are originally used to support navigation on finding directions.

Pakistan is home to a rich tapestry of landmarks that reflect its diverse history, culture, and natural beauty. From ancient civilizations to modern architectural marvels, the country's landmarks tell stories of its past and present.

Pakistan's some famous landmarks are:

"Lahore Fort" , "Shalimar Gardens" , "Badshahi Mosque" , "Faisal Mosque" , "Mohenjo-Daro" , "Quaid-e-Azam’s Mausoleum" "Khyber Pass" , "Hunza Valley" , "Makli Necropolis" , "Minar-e-Pakistan" etc.

The Lahore Fort is located at the northern end of the Walled City of Lahore in Pakistan and spreads over an area greater than 20 hectares. It contains 21 notable monuments, some of which date to the era of Emperor Akbar.....

Mazar-e-Quaid, also known as Jinnah Mausoleum, is a significant monument in Karachi, Pakistan, serving as the final resting place of Muhammad Ali Jinnah, the founder of Pakistan. The mausoleum, completed in 1971, is an iconic symbol of Karachi and one of the city's most popular tourist attractions....

Hiran Minar or "The Deer Tower" is an early 16th-century Mughal era complex in Sheikhupura, in the Pakistani province of Punjab. It was built at the site of a game reserve in honor of Mughal Emperor Jahangir's beloved antelope, Mansraj (lord of all animal beings)....

The Mohatta Palace is a museum located in Karachi, Sindh, Pakistan. Designed by Ahmed Hussain Agha, it was built in 1927 as the summer home of Shivratan Mohatta, a Hindu Marwari businessman from Rajasthan.....

Derawar Fort is a fortress located in Ahmadpur East Tehsil of Bahawalpur District, Punjab, Pakistan. Approximately 20 km south of Ahmedpur East, the fort's forty bastions are visible across the Cholistan Desert. The walls have a perimeter of 1500 meters and stand up to thirty meters high....

Ranikot Fort, also known as the Great Wall of Sindh, is a 19th-century (reconstructed) Talpur-era fort near Sann in Jamshoro District, Sindh, Pakistan. The fort's ramparts have been compared to the Great Wall of China. The site was nominated for UNESCO World Heritage status in 1993 and is on the tentative list. It is protected under the Antiquities Act, 1975 and its amendments....

The Shalimar Gardens are a Mughal garden complex located in Lahore, Punjab, Pakistan. Dating from the period when the Mughal Empire was at its artistic and aesthetic zenith, the gardens are now one of Pakistan's most popular tourist destinations. The Shalimar Gardens were designed as a Persian paradise garden to represent an earthly utopia where humans co-exist harmoniously with nature. Construction began in 1641 during the reign of Emperor Shah Jahan and was completed in 1642. In 1981, the gardens were inscribed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site for their embodiment of Mughal garden design at its peak....

The Tomb of Jahangir is a 17th-century mausoleum built for Mughal Emperor Jahangir. Constructed in 1637, it is located in Shahdara Bagh near Lahore, Pakistan, along the banks of the Ravi River. The site is famous for its richly decorated pietra dura exteriors and interiors embellished with frescoes and marble. The tomb, along with the adjacent Akbari Sarai and the Tomb of Asif Khan, is on the tentative list for UNESCO World Heritage status.....

The Hunza Valley is a mountainous valley in the northern part of the Gilgit-Baltistan region of Pakistan. The valley lies along the Hunza River and borders Ishkoman to the northwest, Shigar to the southeast, Afghanistan's Wakhan Corridor to the north, and the Xinjiang region of China to the northeast. The Hunza Valley floor is at an elevation of 2,438 meters (7,999 feet). Geographically, the Hunza Valley consists of three regions: Upper Hunza (Gojal), Central Hunza, and Lower Hunza (Shinaki)....

Saiful Muluk is a mountainous lake in northern Pakistan, located at the northern end of the Kaghan Valley, near the town of Naran in the Saiful Muluk National Park. At an elevation of 3,224 meters (10,578 feet) above sea level, it is one of the highest lakes in Pakistan. The lake is situated in the Mansehra district of Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, about 9 kilometers (5.6 miles) north of Naran. Malika Parbat, the highest peak in the valley, is near the lake....

The Faisal Mosque is the national mosque of Pakistan, located in the capital city, Islamabad. It is the fifth-largest mosque in the world, the largest outside the Middle East, and the largest in South Asia, located on the foothills of Margalla Hills. It is named after the late King Faisal of Saudi Arabia. The mosque features a contemporary design consisting of eight sides of concrete shell, inspired by a typical Bedouin tent.....

Minar-e-Pakistan is a tower located in Lahore, Punjab, Pakistan. The tower was built between 1960 and 1968 on the site where the Lahore Resolution (later called the Pakistan Resolution) was passed on 23 March 1940 – the first official call for a separate and independent homeland for the Muslims of British India, as espoused by the two-nation theory. The resolution eventually led to the creation of Pakistan in 1947.....

Arts And Crafts

Pakistani Art has a long tradition and history, encompassing various art forms, including painting, sculpture, calligraphy, pottery, and textile arts such as woven silk. Pakistani Craft is a traditional art of the Pakistani people, involving the production, design, or shaping of objects using simple tools or by hand. Crafted by individuals, groups, or independent artists, these works often involve traditional materials like brass, wood, clay, textiles, and paper. Stone carving, metalwork, pottery, and ajrak are commonly used techniques in Pakistani handicrafts.

After independence in 1947, Pakistan had only two major art schools: the Mayo School of Art and the Department of Fine Arts at Punjab University. Early pioneers of Pakistani art include Abdur Rahman Chughtai, known for his Mughal and Islamic styles, and Ahmed Parvez, an early modernist.

In the 1960s and 1970s, calligraphic styles became prominent, with notable artists like Iqbal Geoffrey and Sadequain. The Karachi School of Art, the first art institution in Karachi, was founded in 1964 by Rabia Zuberi.

In the 21st century, graffiti gained popularity, with artists like Sanki King and Asim Butt emerging. The latter also led the Stuckism movement in Pakistan. Pakistani handicrafts have a rich history, though their exact origins are uncertain. Despite flourishing during the Indus Valley civilization, dates and origins remain unclear. Experts claim that 80% of South Asian craftsmanship is still found in Pakistan, with many former craftsmen hailing from different regions of the country.

National Festivals

Pakistan celebrates several national festivals throughout the year to commemorate historic events, promote cultural values, and foster national unity. These festivals hold significant importance in the lives of Pakistanis, as they showcase the rich cultural diversity and heritage of the country.

Independence Day

Independence Day is celebrated on the 14th of August to commemorate Pakistan's independence from British rule in 1947. The day begins with a flag-hoisting ceremony, followed by a national anthem and a 21-gun salute. People decorate their homes, cars, and streets with flags and green and white lights. Cultural shows, parades, and fireworks displays are also held across the country. The highlight of the day is the President's address to the nation, which is broadcasted on national television and radio.
