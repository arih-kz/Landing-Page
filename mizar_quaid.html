The design of the Mazar-e-Quaid is a blend of 1960s modernist architecture, influenced by the Samanid Mausoleum in Bukhara, Uzbekistan, which dates back to between 892 and 943 CE. The mausoleum complex also houses the tombs of Jinnah's sister, Fatima Jinnah, and the first and eighth Prime Ministers of Pakistan, Liaquat Ali Khan and Nurul Amin, respectively.

The process of selecting a design for the mausoleum was complex and involved multiple architects. Initially, in 1954, an Indian architect was selected but later dismissed. In 1955, a Turkish architect's plan was also rejected.

The Government of Pakistan then held an international competition in 1957, which was won by British architect William Whitfield. However, Fatima Jinnah, who had a strong influence over the project, vetoed the design and took control of the process. She ultimately commissioned Yahya Merchant, a Bombay-based architect and a personal friend of Jinnah, to design the monument.

The foundation stone of the mausoleum was laid by President Ayub Khan on 31 July 1960, and the monument was inaugurated by President Yahya Khan on 18 January 1971. The surrounding gardens were not completed until 24 December 2000.
